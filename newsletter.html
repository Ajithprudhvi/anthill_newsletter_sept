The Indian startup ecosystem continues to evolve rapidly, with distinct momentum across sectors. In consumer tech, D2C brands and lifestyle startups are leaning into hyper-personalization, community-driven engagement, and premium, experiential products that resonate with the aspirational Indian consumer. Healthcare innovation is witnessing a surge, particularly in preventive care, mental health, and AI-driven diagnostics, reflecting a growing focus on holistic well-being.

Meanwhile, sustainability is no longer a niche - it is becoming central to product design, packaging, and supply chains, as startups embrace circular models and eco-conscious offerings to meet both regulatory pressure and conscious consumer demand.

Together, these trends highlight an Indian market that is increasingly discerning, digitally native, and purpose-driven, creating fertile ground for startups that can innovate with speed, credibility, and impact.

Our portfolio companies have been busy building, expanding, and strengthening their market presence. Here's a quick look at their progress this quarter.

Portfolio Updates

Building, expanding, and strengthening market presence across sectors

Moonshine Meadery

Moonshine continues to show steady progress with business metrics improving and losses reducing significantly this quarter. While dispatches in May and June were limited due to operational reasons, the company made a significant milestone by opening its second international market - the UK.

A combination of brand partnerships and contract manufacturing now contributes nearly 30% of revenues, helping reduce burn and moving the company closer to profitability. Moonshine is targeting $2M in revenues this year with a bottom line slightly in the black.

For Q2, the focus is on opening 30 new tap accounts in Mumbai and Pune to revive the draft business, strengthening distribution partner brands like Animal, Suru, and Malabar in Maharashtra and Haryana, and expanding white-label opportunities in Mumbai/Pune.

Amar Chitra Katha (ACK)

ACK reported strong performance across multiple channels in August 2025, with reseller and institutional sales reaching ₹95 lakhs, achieving 88% of YTD targets. Major orders included 10,000 copies supplied to EnPower Learning for government school programs, a Kannada edition order from the Azim Premji Foundation, and supplies to over 477 schools through a Delhi distributor.

The company continues to push regional editions with 26 titles under printing and 24 under translation. E-commerce showed healthy momentum with bulk orders, festive season POs from Flipkart and Blinkit, and a government e-book order for 168 titles contributing ₹17 lakhs in digital revenue.

On the brand stories front, ACK secured notable partnerships including Applause (₹75 lakhs), KIIT (₹7 lakhs), Tata Housing (₹4 lakhs), and CDSL (₹3.93 lakhs), totaling nearly ₹91.5 lakhs. Overall, ACK remains well positioned to benefit from festive demand, digital growth, and strong brand partnerships in the coming months.

Climate and Sustainability

10 years since the Paris Agreement and we've seen one great mindset shift in the Indian VC ecosystem. Investing in renewable energy, storage and infrastructure is not niche anymore. From Deep Tech investments that focus on emerging innovations in energy storage and alternate materials and VC firms focusing on Climate/energy as a dedicated sector, to the emergence of newer, purely climate-focused funds, Clean Energy is the next big thing.

Anthill is actively focusing on sourcing leading startups in energy storage and electrification solutions that will help supercharge India's net-zero goals and form the backbone of our rapidly developing economy.
